Our Story

It has long been a dream of mine to run a flower farm and use the flowers to create captivating arrangements. I was inspired from a very young age with both my parents having a love of gardening and nature.

In 2006 I finally became a grown up!
I bought a house with my husband in Broadhembury which had a large garden. I set about turning it into my dream garden, filling it with cuttings I scavenged from my Mum! Many years, and a son, later, I think I’m nearly there. I say, “nearly there”; as any gardener will admit, there is always more to do! But I have the perennial flowers, the foliage, and the polytunnel bursting with seedlings that I had always envisaged. And I have that feeling of satisfaction as I step back and look at what I, and nature, have created

But this haven of greenery didn’t soften my dreams of owning a flower farm. It just made me imagine bigger!

Alongside my hobby of gardening, I have been privileged to have a long and very fulfilling career as a cancer nurse practitioner. But the Covid pandemic made me pause and question my day-to-day. My garden has, and always will be, a place for me to feel calm and clear my head. During those lockdown months I reminisced about my youth when my brother and I would while away hours in the garden, unconsciously absorbing the plant names as our Mum brought her passion to life in the garden that she too longingly dreamed of in her younger years. My Dad was equally passionate about the outdoors, often regaling us with stories of his upbringing in a world of home grown fruit and vegetables.

...

Growing and tending to plants is part of my DNA and lockdown gave me time to truly reflect on how much joy it brings me.

It made me realise that the haven of colour, shapes, and movement I created in my garden was just the first chapter.

It made me imagine bigger and start to work turning my dream into the reality of Wood Lane Flowers.

Yes, it can be a harsh reality at times - backbreaking digging, relentless weather forecast watching, and battling the thoughts of self doubt, but the results are most definitely worth it.

Today, in addition to my garden, I have an additional plot of land a stone's throw away which is a sanctuary for wildlife, protected by beautiful old trees.

Between these spaces I grow a large selection of foliage, perennial, annual, and biennial flowers. And if that isn’t enough, I get to harvest these gems of nature and convert them into displays that other people enjoy. It’s been a big shift from the career I knew for so long, but running Wood Lane Flowers gives me a true sense of contentment which I am thank my lucky stars for on a regular basis.
